Angono, Rizal

The Art Capital of the Philippines

The town is home to the Angono-Binangonan Petroglyphs, considered the oldest known work of art in the Philippines, dating back to before 2000 BC. Lakeside Views of Angono

Angono's position along the northern shores of Laguna de Bay provides some of the most picturesque waterfront views in Rizal province. The lake, which is the largest in the Philippines, has shaped Angono's culture and economy for centuries. Local fishermen still use traditional techniques passed down through generations, casting nets from wooden bancas at dawn.

The lakeside area comes alive at sunset when the water reflects vibrant hues of orange and purple. This natural spectacle has inspired countless artworks by local painters. Several lakeside restaurants offer fresh seafood dishes like sinigang na hipon (shrimp sour soup) and inihaw na tilapia (grilled tilapia), allowing visitors to enjoy the view while sampling authentic flavors.

Environmental conservation efforts led by local artists and community groups help maintain the lake's beauty. Annual clean-up drives and ecological art installations raise awareness about protecting this vital natural resource.

The Higantes Festival: A Cultural Spectacle

Every November, Angono transforms into a vibrant parade ground for its famous Higantes Festival. The highlight are the enormous papier-mâché puppets (higantes) that tower over spectators, some reaching heights of 12-15 feet. These colorful figures originated as satirical representations of abusive Spanish hacienda owners during colonial times.

The festival honors San Clemente, the patron saint of fishermen, blending Catholic tradition with indigenous cultural expressions. Preparations begin months in advance, with different barangays competing to create the most impressive higantes. The parade features not just the giants but also marching bands, street dancers in elaborate costumes, and decorated floats.

Visitors can witness the intricate process of higantes-making in local workshops year-round. The art form has evolved to include contemporary themes while maintaining traditional techniques using bamboo frames, newspaper, and glue.

Angono's Culinary Heritage

Angono's food culture reflects its artistic soul and lakeside location. The town is particularly famous for sinaing na tulingan (braised tuna), cooked slowly in clay pots with banana leaves, garlic, and batwan fruit for a uniquely tangy flavor. This dish represents the perfect marriage of indigenous cooking techniques and Spanish influences.

Other must-try specialties include:

  • Crispy pata - Deep-fried pork knuckle with a golden crackling skin
  • Inihaw na hito - Grilled catfish served with a special soy-vinegar dip
  • Puto bumbong - Purple rice cakes steamed in bamboo tubes, a Christmas tradition

The annual "Kainan sa Dapit-Hapon" food festival showcases these delicacies alongside cooking demonstrations and culinary competitions that highlight Angono's rich gastronomic traditions.

Street Murals: Angono's Living Canvas

Walking through Angono's streets feels like visiting an open-air gallery. The town's walls serve as canvases for breathtaking murals that depict local history, folklore, and scenes of daily life. These artworks aren't just decorations - they're visual stories that preserve community memories and values.

Notable mural sites include:

  • The Botong Francisco Mural on Doña Aurora Street, recreating the National Artist's iconic works
  • The Fishermen's Wall along Laguna de Bay shoreline, celebrating the town's fishing heritage
  • The Children's Art Corridor featuring works by young Angono artists

Local artists frequently organize mural-painting workshops where visitors can contribute to community art projects. The municipal government supports these initiatives through the "Pintura Angono" program, ensuring the town's artistic legacy continues to flourish.

Angono: The Art Capital's Historical Journey

Angono's artistic legacy stretches back millennia, evidenced by the Angono-Binangonan Petroglyphs discovered in 1965 by National Artist Carlos "Botong" Francisco. These ancient rock carvings, dating back to circa 3000 BC, feature 127 human and animal figures and represent the Philippines' oldest known work of art.

Colonial Era Development

During Spanish rule, Angono became known for its skilled artisans who created religious sculptures and church decorations. The town's St. Clement Parish Church houses centuries-old santos (saint statues) carved by local craftsmen. This religious art tradition evolved into the vibrant contemporary scene Angono is famous for today.

Birthplace of National Artists

Angono takes immense pride as the hometown of two Philippine National Artists:

  • Carlos "Botong" Francisco (1912-1969) - Master muralist who pioneered the Filipino imagery in visual arts
  • Maestro Lucio San Pedro (1913-2002) - Celebrated composer known as the "Father of Philippine Impressionist Music"

Modern Cultural Renaissance

Today, Angono sustains its artistic reputation through numerous galleries, the annual Art Festival, and community art programs. The Blanco Family Museum showcases the works of the renowned Blanco family of painters, while the Nemiranda Art House offers workshops and artist residencies. Angono's creative energy continues to inspire new generations of Filipino artists.
